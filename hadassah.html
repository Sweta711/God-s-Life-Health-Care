Hadassah Medical Center, Jerusalem, Israel

  • Founded in 1934, operates two university hospitals: Ein Kerem and Mount Scopus.
  • Has approximately 1,300-1,413 beds across both campuses.
  • Staff includes ~850 physicians, ~1,940 nurses, and ~1,020 paramedical & support staff.
  • Includes 31 operating theaters and 9 specialized intensive care units.

About Hospital

  • Hadassah Medical Center is a major teaching hospital and medical research center in Jerusalem.
  • Operates two university hospital campuses—Ein Kerem and Mount Scopus—serving both local and national patients.
  • Provides over 1,000 beds, numerous specialized departments, and advanced diagnostics and therapeutic facilities.
  • Renowned for its research achievements, medical education programs, and commitment to serving all patients regardless of background.

Amenities

  • Comprehensive outpatient clinics, including specialized care in surgery, oncology, pediatrics, and transplant medicine.
  • Full emergency services and trauma care.
  • Sharap Service – private/choice service allowing patients to select senior physicians.
  • Modern facilities including the Sarah Wetsman Davidson Tower with advanced operating, ICU, imaging, and teaching zones.
  • Educational and research affiliation with Hebrew University; schools of medicine, dentistry, nursing, pharmacology, allied medical professions.
