Cairo University Hospitals (Kasr El Aini), Egypt

  • Founded in 1827 and affiliated with the Faculty of Medicine, Cairo University; relocated to the Kasr El Aini site in 1837.
  • Comprises multiple hospitals under Cairo University, including the New Kasr El Aini Teaching Hospital with around 1,200 beds and over 5,200 beds in total.
  • Handles millions of patients annually — approximately 7.3 million outpatient and emergency visits each year.
  • Renowned for its medical education, research, and healthcare services across all specialties, including surgery, internal medicine, pediatrics, and obstetrics & gynecology.

About Hospital

  • Kasr El Aini Hospital was originally founded in 1827, one of the oldest teaching hospitals in the Middle East and Africa.
  • It serves as the main clinical training center for the Faculty of Medicine, Cairo University.
  • Renovation and modernization projects are ongoing to expand facilities and integrate specialized departments.
  • The hospital provides high-quality services in surgery, cardiology, oncology, radiology, and emergency medicine.

Amenities

  • Comprehensive inpatient and outpatient departments.
