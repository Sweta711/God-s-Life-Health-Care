About Hospital

  • Johns Hopkins Hospital was founded in 1889 through the bequest of philanthropist Johns Hopkins.
  • It is a world-class teaching hospital and research center, closely tied with Johns Hopkins School of Medicine.
  • The hospital has been a pioneer in medicine: early use of rubber surgical gloves, “grand rounds,” and many clinical innovations.
  • Facilities include the Zayed Tower & Bloomberg Children’s Center, added in 2012.

Amenities

  • Comprehensive outpatient & inpatient services.
  • Children’s care through the Bloomberg Children's Center.
  • Level I trauma services.
  • State-of-the-art diagnostic, surgical, and emergency departments.
  • Part of a global enterprise Johns Hopkins Medicine offering research & education worldwide.
