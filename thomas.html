St. Thomas'Hospital, London, UK

  • Founded in the 12th century and named after St. Thomas Becket, one of England's oldest hospitals.
  • Relocated to its current site on the south bank of the River Thames in 1871, opposite the Houses of Parliament.
  • Has around 840 inpatient beds and employs more than 12,500 staff under Guy's and St. Thomas' NHS Foundation Trust.
  • Affiliated with King’s College London and serves as a major teaching and research hospital.

About Hospital

  • St. Thomas’ Hospital has a history dating back over 900 years, making it one of the oldest medical institutions in the UK.
  • The hospital was rebuilt at its current location in 1871 and has since become a key part of London’s healthcare network.
  • It is home to the Evelina London Children’s Hospital, providing specialist pediatric care.
  • The hospital is globally recognized for excellence in cardiology, cancer services, obstetrics, intensive care, and organ transplantation.
  • It plays a major role in training medical students from King’s College London and contributes extensively to clinical research.
